Synopsis: In "The foster-sisters: A story in the days of Wesley and Whitfield," Lucy Ellen Guernsey takes readers on a captivating journey through the lives of two young women, Mary and Emily, who are brought together by fate and their shared experiences. Set in the era of John Wesley and George Whitfield, this historical novel delves into the challenges and triumphs faced by these foster-sisters as they navigate their way through a world filled with societal expectations, religious fervor, and personal growth. As their paths intertwine, Mary and Emily find themselves grappling with love, faith, and the pursuit of their own individual destinies. Will they be able to overcome the obstacles that stand in their way and forge their own paths in a world that seeks to confine them? Genres: Historical fiction, Romance, Coming-of-age. Themes: Identity, Religion, Female empowerment.
