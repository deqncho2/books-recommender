Synopsis: In "A Little Dinner at Timmins's," Thackeray takes us into the world of a seemingly ordinary dinner party, where the guests' true colors are revealed through their conversations and interactions. As the evening unfolds, we witness the subtle power dynamics, social pretenses, and hidden agendas that lie beneath the surface of polite society. With his sharp wit and keen observations, Thackeray invites us to question the authenticity of human relationships and the masks we wear in the pursuit of social success.

Genres: Satire, Social commentary, Short story

Themes: Social class, Hypocrisy, Deception, Authenticity
