Synopsis: In the second volume of "Personal Recollections of Joan of Arc," Mark Twain continues his captivating retelling of the extraordinary life of Joan of Arc. As Joan's journey unfolds, we witness her unwavering determination, her remarkable leadership, and her unyielding faith in the face of adversity. Twain's vivid storytelling transports us to the heart of medieval France, where Joan's courage and conviction inspire a nation and challenge the established order. This volume delves deeper into Joan's trials and triumphs, revealing the complexities of her character and the profound impact she had on history.

Genres: Historical fiction, Biography, Adventure

Themes: Heroism, Faith, Gender roles, Power and authority
