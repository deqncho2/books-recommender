Synopsis: In 'Under Western Eyes' by Joseph Conrad, the reader is transported to the tumultuous world of pre-revolutionary Russia. The story follows the life of Razumov, a young Russian student who becomes entangled in a web of political intrigue and betrayal. When he is forced to make a life-altering decision, Razumov finds himself torn between loyalty and self-preservation, as he navigates a dangerous landscape of spies, revolutionaries, and secret societies. As the plot unfolds, the reader is taken on a gripping journey through the complexities of human nature and the consequences of one's choices.

Genres: Psychological fiction, Political thriller, Historical fiction

Themes: Betrayal, Identity, Morality, Political oppression, Revolution
