Synopsis: 
Sara Crewe: or, What Happened at Miss Minchin's Boarding School is a captivating novel that follows the extraordinary journey of Sara Crewe, a young girl who finds herself in a world of privilege and then suddenly plunged into poverty. Sent to Miss Minchin's boarding school in London, Sara's life takes a dramatic turn when her father's fortune is lost, leaving her at the mercy of a cruel headmistress. Despite the hardships she faces, Sara's resilience and imagination shine through as she navigates the challenges of her new life, finding solace in the power of friendship and the belief in the magic of storytelling.

Genres: Children's literature, Bildungsroman, Historical fiction
Themes: Resilience, Imagination, Friendship, Class and social hierarchy
