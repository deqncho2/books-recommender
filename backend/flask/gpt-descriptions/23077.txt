Synopsis: 
Jane Eyre, a young orphan, endures a difficult childhood filled with mistreatment and isolation. As she grows older, Jane's resilience and intelligence lead her to become a governess at Thornfield Hall, a mysterious and imposing estate. There, she encounters the enigmatic Mr. Rochester, the brooding master of the house, and finds herself drawn to him despite his dark secrets. Jane must navigate the complexities of love, societal expectations, and her own sense of self-worth as she embarks on a journey of self-discovery and independence.

Genres: 
Gothic fiction, Romance, Bildungsroman

Themes: 
Love and passion, Social class and gender roles, Individuality and independence
