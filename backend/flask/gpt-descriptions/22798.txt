Synopsis: 
In 'The Book of Job', a man named Job, who is wealthy and righteous, suddenly finds himself facing unimaginable suffering and loss. As he grapples with his misfortune, Job engages in a profound and intense dialogue with his friends, exploring the nature of human suffering, the existence of evil, and the concept of divine justice. Through his relentless search for answers, Job's faith is tested to its limits, leading to a thought-provoking exploration of the human condition and the complexities of faith.

Genres: Religious literature, philosophical fiction, allegory

Themes: Suffering and loss, theodicy (the problem of evil), faith and doubt, divine justice
