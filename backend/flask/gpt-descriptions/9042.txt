Synopsis: 
'Extracts from Adam's Diary' by Mark Twain is a humorous and insightful account of the first man's experiences in the Garden of Eden. Through Adam's witty and often perplexed observations, we witness his encounters with various creatures, his growing curiosity about the world around him, and his evolving relationship with Eve. As Adam navigates the complexities of life in paradise, his musings offer a fresh perspective on human nature, love, and the pursuit of knowledge.

Genres: 
Humor, Satire, Fiction

Themes: 
Human nature, Love, Curiosity, Knowledge
