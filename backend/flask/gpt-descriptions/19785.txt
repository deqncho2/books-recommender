Synopsis: 
In "Declaration of Independence of the United States of America," Thomas Jefferson presents a powerful and impassioned argument for the separation of the American colonies from British rule. Through eloquent prose and a compelling narrative, Jefferson explores the fundamental rights of individuals and the inherent duty to resist tyranny. This seminal document not only serves as a declaration of independence but also lays the groundwork for the principles upon which the United States of America was built.

Genres: Political philosophy, Historical document

Themes: Freedom, Equality, Individual rights, Resistance to tyranny
