Synopsis: 
In Plato's "Euthyphro," a young man named Euthyphro finds himself entangled in a moral dilemma as he prepares to prosecute his own father for murder. Seeking guidance, Euthyphro encounters the renowned philosopher Socrates, and their conversation delves into the nature of piety, justice, and the gods. As the dialogue unfolds, Euthyphro's certainty is challenged, and Socrates skillfully dismantles his arguments, leading both characters on a thought-provoking journey of self-discovery and philosophical inquiry.

Genres: 
Philosophical dialogue, Drama

Themes: 
Piety, Justice, Morality, Knowledge, Socratic Method
