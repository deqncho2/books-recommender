Synopsis: In "To Be Read at Dusk," Charles Dickens takes readers on a mysterious journey through the streets of London as a man encounters a peculiar stranger with a haunting story to tell. As the night unfolds, secrets are revealed, and the line between reality and imagination becomes blurred. With an eerie atmosphere and unexpected twists, this short story will keep readers on the edge of their seats, questioning what is real and what is merely a figment of the imagination.

Genres: Mystery, Gothic Fiction, Psychological Thriller

Themes: Perception vs. Reality, Identity, The Power of Storytelling
