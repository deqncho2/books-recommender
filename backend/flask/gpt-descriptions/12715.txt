Synopsis: 
In "Danny Deever" by Rudyard Kipling, the reader is introduced to a group of soldiers who witness a disturbing event unfold. As they grapple with the aftermath, questions arise about loyalty, duty, and the consequences of one's actions. The poem delves into the complexities of human nature and the impact of societal norms on individuals. 

Genres: 
Poetry, War literature

Themes: 
Loyalty, Duty, Consequences, Morality
