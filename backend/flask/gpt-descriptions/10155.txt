Synopsis: In "The Right of the People to Rule," Theodore Roosevelt presents a compelling argument that challenges the traditional notions of political power and advocates for a more inclusive democracy. Through his powerful rhetoric and insightful analysis, Roosevelt explores the importance of active citizen participation and the need for a government that truly represents the will of the people. As he delves into the complexities of American politics, he raises thought-provoking questions about the role of money, corruption, and the influence of special interests. With a passionate call to action, Roosevelt's work serves as a rallying cry for a more equitable and just society.

Genres: Political philosophy, Non-fiction

Themes: Democracy, Citizen participation, Political corruption, Government accountability
