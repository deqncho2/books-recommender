Synopsis: In 'De Profundis', Oscar Wilde takes readers on a deeply introspective journey as he reflects on his life, imprisonment, and the transformative power of suffering. Through a series of heartfelt letters, Wilde explores themes of love, art, forgiveness, and the pursuit of personal redemption. As he delves into the depths of his soul, the reader is captivated by Wilde's profound insights and his unyielding spirit in the face of adversity.

Genres: Autobiography, Epistolary, Philosophical

Themes: Suffering and Redemption, Art and Aesthetics, Love and Forgiveness
