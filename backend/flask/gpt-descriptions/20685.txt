Synopsis: 
In 'Poetics', Aristotle delves into the art of storytelling, exploring the intricacies of tragedy and epic poetry. Drawing from his extensive knowledge of Greek drama, he dissects the elements that make a compelling narrative, from the structure of a plot to the importance of character development. Through insightful analysis and thought-provoking examples, Aristotle offers invaluable guidance to both aspiring writers and avid readers, shedding light on the timeless principles that underlie the creation of powerful stories.

Genres: Literary criticism, Philosophy

Themes: Tragedy, Epic poetry, Plot structure, Character development
