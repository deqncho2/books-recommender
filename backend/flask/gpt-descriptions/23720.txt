Synopsis: 
In "To Lesbia," Catullus, Gaius Valerius, takes us on a passionate and tumultuous journey through the highs and lows of a passionate love affair. Through his heartfelt and often raw poetry, Catullus explores the depths of desire, the pain of betrayal, and the intoxicating power of love. As he pours his emotions onto the page, the reader is captivated by the intensity and vulnerability of his words, leaving them eager to uncover the outcome of this passionate love story.

Genres: Poetry, Love Poetry, Roman Literature

Themes: Love, Desire, Betrayal, Passion, Vulnerability
