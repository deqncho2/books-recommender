Synopsis: 
In "The Letters of Mark Twain," readers are granted a rare glimpse into the private correspondence of one of America's most beloved authors. Spanning over six decades, this collection of letters showcases Twain's wit, humor, and sharp observations on a wide range of topics. From his travels around the world to his thoughts on politics and society, Twain's letters offer a captivating and intimate portrait of the man behind the iconic characters he created.

Genres: 
Biography, Autobiography, Epistolary

Themes: 
Humor, Social Commentary, Literary Legacy
