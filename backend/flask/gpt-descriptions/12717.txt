Synopsis: 
When Lilacs Last in the Dooryard Bloom'd is a poignant and introspective poem by Walt Whitman that explores the profound impact of loss and grief on the human spirit. Through vivid and evocative imagery, the poem takes readers on a journey of mourning, reflection, and ultimately, acceptance. As the narrator grapples with the death of a beloved figure, they find solace and healing in the beauty of nature and the eternal cycle of life and death. This deeply moving work captures the universal experience of loss and the power of love to transcend even the darkest of times.

Genres: Poetry, Elegy, American Literature

Themes: Grief and Loss, Nature and Transcendence, Love and Remembrance
