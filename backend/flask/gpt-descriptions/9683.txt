Synopsis: In 'A Journal of the Plague Year', Daniel Defoe takes us back to the haunting streets of London during the devastating outbreak of the bubonic plague in 1665. Through the eyes of an unnamed narrator, we witness the horrors and chaos that engulfed the city, as well as the resilience and humanity that emerged amidst the despair. This gripping account offers a vivid portrayal of the fear, desperation, and moral dilemmas faced by individuals in the face of an unstoppable epidemic.

Genres: Historical fiction, Journal, Horror

Themes: Survival, Morality, Human resilience, Societal breakdown
