Synopsis: 

In "Sketches by Boz, illustrative of everyday life and every-day people," Charles Dickens takes us on a captivating journey through the bustling streets of 19th-century London. Through a series of vivid and humorous sketches, Dickens introduces us to a colorful cast of characters, from street vendors and pickpockets to eccentric aristocrats and hardworking laborers. Each sketch offers a glimpse into the lives and struggles of these ordinary individuals, highlighting the stark contrasts and peculiarities of Victorian society. With his keen eye for detail and sharp wit, Dickens paints a captivating portrait of everyday life, capturing both the charm and the harsh realities of the bustling metropolis.

Genres: Fiction, Satire, Social Commentary

Themes: Social inequality, Human nature, Urban life, Satire
