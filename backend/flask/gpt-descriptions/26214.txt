Synopsis: 
'The Jesuit Missions: A Chronicle of the Cross in the Wilderness' by Thomas Guthrie Marquis is a captivating historical account that delves into the extraordinary efforts of the Jesuit missionaries in the wilderness. Set against the backdrop of the untamed American frontier, this chronicle unveils the challenges, triumphs, and sacrifices of these brave men who sought to spread their faith amidst adversity. Through vivid storytelling and meticulous research, Marquis brings to life the untold stories of these missionaries, painting a vivid picture of their encounters with indigenous peoples, the struggles they faced, and the profound impact they had on the cultural landscape of the New World.

Genres: Historical non-fiction, Religious history, Biography

Themes: Faith and spirituality, Cultural exchange, Exploration and colonization
