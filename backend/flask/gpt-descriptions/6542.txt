Synopsis: 
In Mary Shelley's "Frankenstein," a young scientist named Victor Frankenstein becomes obsessed with the idea of creating life. Through his experiments, he succeeds in bringing a creature to life, but soon realizes the consequences of his actions. As the creature struggles to find acceptance and understanding in a world that rejects him, Victor is haunted by the consequences of his creation. The story unfolds through a series of letters and narratives, exploring the moral and ethical implications of scientific advancement and the pursuit of knowledge.

Genres: Gothic fiction, Science fiction, Horror

Themes: The dangers of unchecked ambition, the consequences of isolation and rejection, the nature of humanity and responsibility, the limits of scientific progress
