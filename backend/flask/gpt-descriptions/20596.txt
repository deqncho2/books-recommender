Synopsis: In "The Ballad of Reading Gaol," Oscar Wilde takes readers on a haunting journey through the dark corridors of a prison, where he reflects on the harsh realities of life behind bars. Through vivid imagery and poignant storytelling, Wilde explores the human condition, the nature of sin, and the power of empathy. This powerful ballad delves into the depths of despair and redemption, leaving readers with a profound sense of the fragility of life and the enduring power of compassion.

Genres: Poetry, Ballad, Prison Literature

Themes: Human suffering, Sin and guilt, Empathy and compassion
