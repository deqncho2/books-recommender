Synopsis: 
'Fables de La Fontaine, livre second' by Jean de La Fontaine is a collection of fables that captivate readers with their clever and imaginative storytelling. Each fable presents a moral lesson through the use of anthropomorphic animals, bringing to life relatable situations and characters. From a cunning fox to a wise lion, these fables explore the complexities of human nature and the consequences of our actions. With wit and charm, La Fontaine invites readers to reflect on the follies and virtues of society, leaving them entertained and enlightened.

Genres: 
Fables, Poetry, Moral Literature

Themes: 
Human nature, Morality, Wisdom, Consequences
