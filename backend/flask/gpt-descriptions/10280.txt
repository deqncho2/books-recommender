Synopsis: "In de Mornin'" and "Jes Gib Him One Ob Mine" are two captivating short stories by Paul Laurence Dunbar. In "In de Mornin'," we follow the life of a young African American boy named Joe, who faces a difficult decision that could change his future forever. Meanwhile, "Jes Gib Him One Ob Mine" introduces us to Aunt Mandy, a wise and compassionate woman who finds herself caught in a moral dilemma when a stranger arrives at her doorstep. Both stories explore the complexities of race, identity, and the power of human connection.

Genres: Fiction, Short Stories

Themes: Race, Identity, Morality, Compassion
