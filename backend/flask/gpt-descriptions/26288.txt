Synopsis: 
In 'To His Coy Mistress', a passionate and persuasive speaker addresses his beloved, urging her to seize the present moment and embrace their love before time slips away. Through vivid imagery and clever arguments, the speaker attempts to convince his coy mistress to abandon her reservations and indulge in the pleasures of love. This poem explores the complexities of desire, the fleeting nature of time, and the power of persuasion.

Genres: 
Metaphysical poetry, Carpe diem poetry, Love poetry

Themes: 
Love and desire, Time and mortality, Persuasion and seduction
