Synopsis: 
'Early Short Fiction of Edith Wharton' is a captivating collection of short stories that showcases the brilliant storytelling and keen observations of one of America's greatest writers, Edith Wharton. From tales of forbidden love and societal expectations to explorations of the human psyche and the complexities of relationships, each story takes readers on a journey through the intricacies of human nature and the social dynamics of the early 20th century. With her signature wit and insight, Wharton delves into the lives of her characters, revealing the hidden desires, secrets, and struggles that lie beneath the surface of polite society. This collection is a testament to Wharton's mastery of the short story form and her ability to captivate readers with her richly drawn characters and thought-provoking narratives.

Genres: Fiction, Short Stories, Literary Fiction

Themes: Love and Desire, Society and Class, Human Nature, Relationships, Gender Roles
